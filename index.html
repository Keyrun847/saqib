The 4 Laws of Behaviour Change

1
Make it Obvious
Identify cues that trigger habits.
Keep your journal on your desk.
2
Make it Attractive
Make habits appealing using rewards or pairing.
Listen to your favorite music while working out.
3
Make it Easy
Reduce friction to act on a habit.
Start with 2 push-ups instead of 20.
4
Make it Satisfying
Make the outcome feel rewarding.
Mark it on your calendar after completing.

The Habit Loop

Every habit is formed through a loop:

Cue

Trigger

→

Craving

Desire

↓

Reward

Benefit

←

Response

Action

↑

Understanding this loop allows you to reprogram your habits by changing the cues and rewards. By intentionally modifying one part of the loop, you can build better habits that align with your goals.
